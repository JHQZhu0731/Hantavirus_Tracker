Situation snapshot

Notification
ECDC notified 2 May 2026 by the Netherlands through the EU Early Warning and Response System (EWRS).
Setting
Cruise ship in the South Atlantic; Dutch flag; ~149 people on board from 23 nationalities (including passengers and crew from multiple EU/EEA countries).
Cases (6 May)
Seven cases in the hantavirus-associated cluster: three deaths; one person medically evacuated to South Africa and in intensive care; two symptomatic on board; one person diagnosed after disembarking and returning to Switzerland.
Laboratory
Samples from two patients PCR-positive for hantavirus; one additional patient PCR-positive for Andes virus (ANDV). Further laboratory work ongoing.
Working hypothesis
Some travellers may have been exposed to ANDV in Argentina before embarkation (endemic area), with subsequent transmission to others on board during close, prolonged contact in a closed setting.
EU/EEA population risk
ECDC: risk to the general EU/EEA population from spread off the ship is assessed as very low when infection prevention and control measures are applied; ANDV rodent reservoir is not expected to establish in Europe.

Decision tree for investigators and clinicians

Each row is one branch: if the signal applies, take the control step. Teaching schematic from ECDC's preliminary assessment — not a substitute for national protocols or shipboard medical orders.

# If you see… Then do… ECDC framing
1Unexplained cluster of severe febrile illness with respiratory and/or gastrointestinal features on a passenger vessel, especially after time ashore in South America.Treat as a public health event: activate ship medical surge, line list, exposure history (ports, excursions, cabin contacts), and notify competent authorities per flag state, port state, and international health regulations as applicable.Matches the initial signal ECDC describes from the Dutch notification.
2Any person develops rapid progression to pneumonia, acute respiratory distress, or shock.Arrange urgent medical evacuation to a facility that can provide intensive supportive care; obtain diagnostic samples before or during transfer.ECDC reports at least four of seven cases progressed rapidly; one evacuee tested hantavirus PCR-positive on 3 May.
3PCR shows hantavirus and/or ANDV, or clinical picture strongly suggests HPS in an appropriate exposure context.Isolate confirmed and suspected cases on board where feasible; cohort nursing; trace and monitor close contacts; reinforce hand hygiene, respiratory etiquette, and distancing.ECDC: ANDV person-to-person transmission is rare but documented with close, prolonged contact.
4Early in investigation, everyone on board shares common indoor spaces and social activities (closed environment).Apply the precautionary principle: consider all on board close contacts until epidemiology refines risk tiers.Explicit in ECDC risk assessment for this cluster.
5Symptomatic patient will disembark or be transferred between jurisdictions.Use PCR and/or serology for diagnosis; communicate that negative tests early in illness may not exclude infection; give written follow-up and reporting instructions.ECDC recommendations; EURL network can assist EU labs lacking ANDV capacity.
6Healthcare facility receives a patient from the ship or an epidemiologically linked contact.Apply standard and droplet precautions; escalate to airborne precautions if aerosol-generating procedures are performed.ECDC IPC guidance for ANDV-compatible illness.
7Media or port communities ask whether shore-side outbreaks are likely in Europe.Cite reservoir ecology: ANDV is not expected to establish in European rodents; focus messaging on case/contact management rather than widespread community contagion.ECDC stresses very low EU/EEA population risk under proper controls.

R₀ & Spread Speed

Orthohantaviruses are primarily rodent-borne. Person-to-person transmission is documented for Andes virus under close-contact conditions. Sin Nombre–type HPS in the U.S. is treated as a dead-end zoonotic spillover.
