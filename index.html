Outbreak timeline

  1. 1 Apr 2026MV Hondius departs Ushuaia, Argentina (147 aboard).
  2. 6 Apr 2026Case 1 (Dutch male, 70) develops fever, headache, diarrhoea on board.
  3. 11 Apr 2026Case 1 dies aboard; respiratory distress. Ship between South Georgia and St. Helena.
  4. 24 Apr 2026Case 2 (Dutch female, 69, wife of Case 1) disembarks at St. Helena with GI symptoms. Body of Case 1 removed.
  5. 25 Apr 2026Case 2 deteriorates on flight to Johannesburg.
  6. 26 Apr 2026Case 2 dies at Johannesburg hospital. Case 3 (British male) evacuated from Ascension Island to South Africa ICU.
  7. 2 May 2026Case 4 (German female) dies on board. South Africa lab confirms hantavirus PCR-positive in Case 3 — first lab confirmation, 21 days after first death.
  8. 3 May 2026WHO announces investigation. Ship moored off Cabo Verde.
  9. 4 May 2026WHO DON599 issued: 7 cases (2 confirmed, 5 suspected), 3 deaths. Dutch woman (Case 2) post-mortem PCR confirmed.
  10. 6 May 2026ECDC assessment published. Swiss man (Case 5) PCR confirmed. Ship heads to Canary Islands.
  11. 7 May 20265 of 8 suspected cases confirmed (WHO briefing). 3 symptomatic passengers transferred to Spain for care.
  12. 8 May 2026WHO confirms 6 of 8 cases laboratory-confirmed (PCR/serology); 0 suspected cases remain on ship. US CDC arranges evacuation flight: Offutt AFB, Omaha → National Quarantine Unit, Univ. Nebraska Medical Center. Argentina's Tierra del Fuego epidemiology director states 'almost zero' chance index cases contracted ANDV in Ushuaia.
  13. 9 May 2026ECDC publishes Rapid Scientific Advice: all 147 aboard classified as high-risk contacts; 42-day quarantine/monitoring (Day 0 = 6 May 2026). Updated totals: 6 confirmed + 2 probable, 0 suspected. WHO Director-General Tedros flies to Spain; meets PM Sánchez and accompanies health/interior ministers to Tenerife command post. Repatriation flights arranged by Germany, France, Belgium, Ireland, Netherlands, US, and UK. Ship (MV Hondius) expected to arrive Granadilla, Tenerife, 10 May.
  14. 10 May 2026MV Hondius arrives Granadilla, Tenerife. Disembarkation begins; Spanish nationals evacuated first. 18 US citizens (17 Americans + 1 British-American) airlifted to Offutt Air Force Base, Nebraska on US government charter. CDC launches Andes virus situation summary page.
  15. 11 May 2026ECDC update: 9 total cases (7 confirmed + 2 probable), 3 deaths. US: 18 passengers evaluated at Univ. Nebraska Medical Center — 1 mildly positive (asymptomatic, biocontainment), 15 in National Quarantine Unit, 2 symptomatic transferred to Emory University biocontainment unit (Atlanta). Full disembarkation complete: 94 people from 19 nationalities repatriated. MV Hondius sails for Rotterdam (~25 crew) for full decontamination. CDC: no cases of Andes virus reported in the US from this outbreak; risk to American public extremely low.

Situation snapshot

Notification
2 May 2026 — WHO notified by UK IHR Focal Point; ECDC notified by Netherlands via EWRS.
Vessel
MV Hondius, Dutch flag, operated by Oceanwide Expeditions. Departed Ushuaia, Argentina 1 April 2026.
Itinerary
Ushuaia → Antarctica → South Georgia → Nightingale Island → Tristan da Cunha → St. Helena → Ascension Island → Cabo Verde → Canary Islands (Spain).
Persons aboard
147 total: 88 passengers + 59 crew; 23 nationalities (EU/EEA countries include BE, DE, ES, FR, GR, IE, NL, PL, PT).
Cases (9 May)
9 cases total; 7 confirmed (PCR/serology); 2 probable; as of 12 May 2026; 3 deaths
Laboratory
ANDV confirmed by PCR/serology in 6 of 8 cases as of 9 May. Cases 3, 2, 5 confirmed earlier (NICD, Netherlands, Switzerland labs). Serology, sequencing and metagenomics ongoing at Institut Pasteur de Dakar. Argentina's Tierra del Fuego epidemiology director: 'almost zero' probability of Ushuaia zoonotic acquisition.
Index cases
Cases 1 and 2 (Dutch couple) had travelled through Argentina, Chile, and Uruguay for ~4 months before boarding — believed primary zoonotic exposure pre-embarkation.
Working hypothesis
Zoonotic acquisition by index cases in South America (endemic Andes virus rodent reservoir); subsequent limited person-to-person transmission in closed-ship setting.
Population risk
ECDC (9 May): EU/EEA general population risk VERY LOW; passengers/crew risk MODERATE. All 147 aboard reclassified as high-risk contacts; 42-day monitoring (Day 0 = 6 May 2026). WHO: global risk LOW; 'not the start of a pandemic.' ANDV reservoir absent from European rodents.

Decision tree for investigators and clinicians

Each row is one branch: if the signal applies, take the control step. Teaching schematic from ECDC’s preliminary assessment — not a substitute for national protocols or shipboard medical orders.

# If you see… Then do… ECDC framing
1Unexplained cluster of severe febrile illness with respiratory / GI features on a passenger vessel, especially after transit through South America.Declare a public health event of concern on board: activate medical surge, create line list, collect exposure histories (ports, excursions, animal contact, close contacts), notify flag state and competent port authority under IHR 2005.Matches the 2 May 2026 UK IHR notification that triggered WHO response (DON599).
2Any person develops fever with rapid progression to pneumonia, ARDS, or shock.Medical evacuation to a facility with full ICU capability as a priority; collect blood/respiratory specimens before or during transfer; notify receiving country.Cases 3 and 4 illustrate rapid deterioration: Case 3 evacuated Ascension → South Africa ICU; Case 4 died 4 days after symptom onset.
3PCR or serology confirms hantavirus / ANDV, or clinical picture is strongly consistent and other pathogens excluded.Cohort-nurse confirmed and suspected cases; strict cabin isolation; trace all close contacts (shared cabin, meals, activities); apply droplet + contact precautions; airborne precautions for aerosol-generating procedures.ECDC: person-to-person ANDV transmission documented with close prolonged contact; all 147 aboard treated as contacts (closed environment).
4Closed vessel environment — everyone shares social spaces and activities.Apply precautionary principle: treat all aboard as close contacts until epidemiological investigation refines risk tiers.Explicit in both WHO DON599 and ECDC assessment for this cluster.
5Patient will disembark or transfer between jurisdictions.Provide written symptom-monitoring instructions for 45 days (up to 8-week incubation possible). Ensure receiving jurisdiction is notified. PCR / serology at time of disembarkation — note that negative tests early in illness do not exclude infection.WHO advises 45-day monitoring window. Swiss case (Case 5) identified post-disembark at St. Helena — underlines need for follow-up.
6Healthcare facility receives a patient from the ship or an epidemiologically linked contact.Standard + droplet precautions for all suspect/confirmed ANDV cases; escalate to airborne precautions for aerosol-generating procedures. Document exposure history; alert IHR focal point if transferring across borders.WHO and ECDC IPC guidance; secondary healthcare-worker infections documented in prior ANDV outbreaks.
7Media, passengers, or shore-side communities ask whether widespread community spread is likely.Cite WHO assessment: global risk low. ANDV rodent reservoir absent from Europe. Transmission requires close, prolonged contact — not airborne spread comparable to COVID-19.WHO Director-General Tedros, 7 May 2026: 'Not the start of a pandemic.' Low EU/EEA population risk affirmed by ECDC.

R₀ & Spread Speed

Orthohantaviruses are primarily rodent-borne. Person-to-person transmission is documented for Andes virus under close-contact conditions. Sin Nombre–type HPS in the U.S. is treated as a dead-end zoonotic spillover.
